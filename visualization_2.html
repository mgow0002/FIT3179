GDP Vs Unemployment Trend

Talking about the trend of two subjects (GDP and Unemployment), there is a great importance of knowing the relationship between these two which can be supported by Okun's law stating that GDP rate must grow so the reduction of the unemployment can be potentially achieved. To prove this perspective correct, Let's make a trend comparison between GDP and Unemployment. In 2019, the Covid-19 occured and has affected the significant number of people around the world and continued doing this inevitably until 2020. There was a slight decline in 2020 due to the existence of Covid-19 which caused great degree of unemployment among a group of society. As time passed by, the situation started to get recovered and the GDP rate increased bit by bit. From 2021 to 2023, the GDP holds the upward trend in general meaning that the unemployment rate will be considerably reduced by no means. Therefore, this concludes the theory made by Oken is true and still produces more positive outcomes.
